══════════════════════════════════════════════════
  EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
══════════════════════════════════════════════════

EvidLY scores a solid B- overall — well above "indie project" but below
"enterprise design team" polish. The app's strengths are its domain expertise,
skeleton loading system, emotional empty state copy, and consistent table/icon
patterns.

The primary issues are:
  1. TWO COMPETING NAVY COLORS (#1e4d6b vs #1E2D4D)
  2. Brand cream (#FAF7F0) barely adopted
  3. 3-4 primary button styles with no shared component
  4. Basic 404 page

These 4 issues account for most of the "looks like one developer" impression.
Fixing them would lift the overall score to a B+, which is where enterprise
SaaS products need to be for Aramark-level buyers.

The good news: the underlying component patterns are solid. This is a
unification and standardization task, not a redesign. The architecture
supports a polished product — it just needs a focused design pass.
